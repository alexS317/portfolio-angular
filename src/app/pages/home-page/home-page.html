About me

I have always had a wide range of interests and enjoyed learning new things and expressing myself creatively. When I was a teenager and had to start thinking about what I wanted to do after school, I decided that I would like to work on computer games, either as an artist or developer. I have always enjoyed playing them (with some of my favourites nowadays being the two Kingdom Come: Deliverance games, Medieval Dynasty, and Stellaris, among many others), and the thought of contributing to the creation of future games was very exciting to me.

After school I applied for the bachelor programme Creative Computing, where got to know various other use cases for programming besides games, and discovered that for example web applications and mobile apps can be super fun to develop as well. By now I have about two years of professional experience as a front-end web developer, and I would be very happy to both deepen my knowledge in this area, as well as expand my skills into other fields.

I want to be open about the fact that I do not use generative AI tools for any of my creative work (writing, illustration, 3D art, sound etc.). For one thing, this is because I am aware that a lot of AI models were trained on artists' work without their consent or any kind of compensation. Plus, I just enjoy the process of creation so much, and it would be quite stupid of me to outsource precisely the fun work to AI, wouldn't you agree?
